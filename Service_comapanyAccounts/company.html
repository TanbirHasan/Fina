Financial Statements
consists of

  • Income Statements(Profit and Loss Statement):

    Summarizes a company's revenues, expenses, and profits over a specific period, typically a fiscal year.

  • Balance Sheet:

    Provides an overview of a company's assets, liabilities, and shareholders' equity at a specific point in time, showcasing its financial position.

  • Cash Flow Statements:

    Tracks the flow of cash in and out of the company, helping to assess liquidity and cash management.

  • Managment disscussion and analysis:

    Provides additional information and explanations about items in the financial statements, offering transparency and context.

  • Audited financial statements:

    Public companies and some private companies are required to have their financial statements audited by independent auditors to ensure accuracy and compliance.
